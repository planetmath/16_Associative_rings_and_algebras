A graded algebra $A$ is said to be a \textbf{superalgebra} if it has a $\Zset/2\Zset$ grading.
As a vector space, a superalgebra has a decomposition into two homogeneous subspaces, $A = A_0\oplus A_1$.
The homogeneous subspace $A_0$ is known as the space of \textbf{even} elements of $A$, and $A_1$ is known as the space of \textbf{odd} elements.
Let $|a|$ denote the degree of a homogeneous element.
That is, $|a| = 0$ if $a \in A_0$ and $|a| = 1$ if $a \in A_1$.
The degree satisfies $|ab| = |a| + |b|$.
