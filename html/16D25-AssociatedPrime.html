associated prime


Let R be a ring, and let M be an R-module. A prime ideal P of R is an for M if P=ann⁢(X), the annihilator of some nonzero submodule X of M.

Note that if this is the case, then the module annM⁢(P) contains X, has P as its annihilator, and is a faithful (http://planetmath.org/FaithfulModule) (R/P)-module.

If, in addition, P is equal to the annihilator of a submodule of M that is a fully faithful (http://planetmath.org/FaithfulModule) (R/P)-module, then we call P an of M.
